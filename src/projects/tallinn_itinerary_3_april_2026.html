Itinerary

9:00 AM to 9:00 PM

9:00 – 10:00

Breakfast at RØST Bakery

Why this works: excellent pastries, specialty coffee, and an easy start before heading into Old Town.

Approx. 20 min walk from your accommodation.

Order
Cardamom bun Sourdough sandwich with smoked fish Flat white or filter coffee

Local note: go early if you want the best pastry selection.

10:00 – 11:15

Pre-tour wander through quiet Old Town corners

Walk toward the meeting point and use the extra time for a gentle warm-up before the tour crowds pick up.

  • St Catherine’s Passage — one of the best hidden medieval lanes in the city.
  • Viru Gates area — a classic Old Town entrance, but nicest before it gets busy.
  • Small courtyards — step through open archways; many are easy to miss and feel more local than the main streets.

Keep the walk to the tour meeting point easy and flexible.

11:30 – 13:45

Your booked walking tour

You’ll cover these spots during the guided portion of the day:

Niguliste tänav (starting point) Danish King’s Garden Alexander Nevsky Cathedral St Mary’s Cathedral Kohtuotsa viewing platform Balti Jaam

Good timing: your tour naturally ends near lunch options and close to the afternoon plan.

13:45 – 15:00

Lunch — pick one of two styles

Both options keep walking short after the tour.

Lunch option 1 · Quick & local

Balti Jaama Turg Food Area

Casual, flexible, and easy for a group of 4 if everyone wants something slightly different.

  • Pelmeni with sour cream
  • Black bread sandwiches
  • Local craft beer
Lunch option 2 · Cozy sit-down

Rataskaevu 16

A warm, reliable Old Town classic with polished Estonian comfort food.

  • Elk or moose stew
  • Creamy mushroom soup
  • Kama dessert
15:00 – 16:30

Telliskivi Creative City

A good energy shift after the medieval core: murals, indie shops, design spaces, and a more modern local vibe.

  • Browse street art and small concept stores
  • Pause for a relaxed coffee if lunch ran quick
  • Optional: pop into Fotografiska Tallinn

Hidden gem: the Fotografiska café/view area can be a smart stop for the atmosphere even if you do not make the museum the main event.

16:30 – 18:00

Chill seaside time at Linnahall

From Telliskivi, use a short public transport hop or an easy walk sequence to keep the day smooth.

  • Brutalist waterfront architecture
  • Open views toward the sea and harbor
  • One of Tallinn’s more local-feeling places to simply sit and take in the city

Why it works: this is the “slow afternoon” moment built into the plan.

18:00 – 18:45

Dessert break at Karjase Sai

A nice reset before dinner rather than rushing straight into the evening.

Seasonal tart Rye-based sweet pastry Specialty coffee

Local specialty angle: rye appears in both savory and sweet Estonian baking, so it is worth trying in dessert form too.

19:00 – 21:00

Dinner — choose your evening mood

Dinner option 1 · Modern Estonian

NOA Chef’s Hall

Best for a more polished, destination-style dinner. Worth a short ride.

  • Tasting menu
  • Baltic fish dishes
  • Seasonal modern Estonian plates
Dinner option 2 · Atmospheric & rustic

III Draakon

A fun Old Town tavern pick if you want memorable atmosphere over refinement.

  • Elk soup
  • Meat pies
  • Pickles from the barrel

Evening finish: by 9 PM you can either return to your accommodation or take a final short Old Town stroll if energy is still high.

Tips

Hidden gems, practical advice, and savings

Hidden gems & local favorites
  • St Catherine’s Passage is one of the best quiet medieval corners before tour groups arrive.
  • Telliskivi gives you a more local, creative side of Tallinn beyond the postcard Old Town.
  • Linnahall is ideal for a calm sea-facing pause and feels less “scheduled” than the main sights.
  • Open courtyards in Old Town are often more charming than the busiest streets.
Practical tips most tourists miss
  • Old Town cobblestones can be slick — wear grippy shoes.
  • April weather can flip quickly, so layers are better than one heavy coat.
  • Early morning is the best time for the prettiest Old Town atmosphere.
  • For a group of 4, casual market-style lunch can save time compared with waiting for a table.
Money-saving hacks · Food & drink
  • Choose lunch menus or simpler midday dishes instead of going big at both lunch and dinner.
  • Drink tap water — Tallinn’s tap water is generally good, so skip bottled water.
Money-saving hacks · Transport
  • Use a 24-hour public transport pass instead of buying multiple single rides.
  • If you need a car, compare Bolt rides with regular taxis; short city hops are often cheaper that way.
Money-saving hacks · Attractions
  • Prioritize free viewpoints like Kohtuotsa instead of stacking paid towers.
  • Use neighborhoods like Telliskivi and the waterfront for atmosphere without needing tickets.
Money-saving hacks · General
  • Tourist souvenir shops in the busiest Old Town lanes are usually pricier than design shops elsewhere.
  • Card payment is widely accepted, so there is little need to exchange much cash.
