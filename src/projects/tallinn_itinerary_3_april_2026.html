One day in Tallinn for Yash, Saurabh, Shruti & Ketki

A relaxed plan built around your 11:30–13:45 walking tour, with short walking stretches, vegetarian-safe choices for Ketki, local favorites, and a calm finish back at the hotel.

👥 Group of 4 🥗 Ketki: pure vegetarian, eggs okay 🚶 Mostly walking + public transport 💶 Meal budget: max €25 pp incl. drink 🏨 Stay: Center Hotel
Day logic
  • Morning: light sightseeing before Old Town gets busy
  • Midday: your walking tour covers the big historic highlights
  • Afternoon: creative district + waterfront chill
  • Evening: dinner near transit, then easy return to hotel
Day plan

9:00 AM to 9:00 PM

9:00 – 10:00

Breakfast at RØST Bakery

Start close to your hotel in the Rotermann area with a calm breakfast before heading into Old Town.

Go to: RØST Bakery VEG / EGG + NON-VEG

  • VEG/EGG cardamom bun or cinnamon bun + coffee
  • VEG/EGG egg sandwich or pastry of the day
  • NON-VEG smoked fish sourdough sandwich

Budget: about €8–14 per person including coffee.

10:00 – 11:15

Quiet Old Town warm-up before the tour crowds

Walk into Old Town and enjoy the prettiest part of the morning before groups fill the streets.

  • Pass through Viru Gates
  • Slip into St Catherine’s Passage for a quieter medieval lane
  • Head toward Niguliste tänav in time for the tour check-in

Hidden gem: small courtyards opening off the main lanes are often more charming than the busiest squares.

11:30 – 13:45

Booked walking tour

Your guide covers the core hilltop and station-side highlights, so you do not need to re-plan these later.

Niguliste tänav (starting point) Danish King’s Garden Alexander Nevsky Cathedral St Mary’s Cathedral Kohtuotsa viewing platform Balti Jaam

Why this flow works: the tour ends near your first lunch option and close to the afternoon chill zone.

13:45 – 15:00

Lunch — choose one of two good-value options

Option 1: Balti Jaama Turg Food Hall VEG + NON-VEG
  • VEG grilled vegetables, soups, falafel/bowl style counters, fresh pastries
  • NON-VEG pelmeni, fish dishes, meat-filled pastries

Budget: €10–18 pp including a soft drink. Best if everyone wants something different.

Option 2: Rukis VEG + NON-VEG
  • VEG rye-bread open sandwiches, soups, cakes, baked goods
  • NON-VEG smoked fish or meat open sandwiches and hearty lunch plates

Budget: €14–22 pp including a soft drink. Good for local breads and an easy seated lunch.

Ketki note: if you want the safest vegetarian lunch, the market has more mix-and-match flexibility; Rukis usually works too, but the market is the easiest no-stress choice.

15:00 – 16:15

Telliskivi Creative City for a slower afternoon

From Balti Jaam it is an easy stroll into Telliskivi Creative City.

  • Street art walls and design corners
  • Courtyards and indie shops with better gift browsing than the tourist-heavy Old Town souvenir lanes
  • Optional quick look at Fotografiska Tallinn

Local favorite: this is where the day starts to feel less “tour checklist” and more local Tallinn.

16:15 – 16:45

Dessert stop in Telliskivi

Go to: Karjase Sai VEG / EGG

  • VEG/EGG seasonal tart, cream bun, rye-based pastry, coffee

Budget: €6–12 pp including coffee. Great pause before the waterfront stretch.

16:45 – 17:45

Easy waterfront chill at Linnahall

Walk or take a short tram/bus hop to Linnahall for sea views and a relaxed hour.

  • One of Tallinn’s more local-feeling places to sit without paying for a ticket
  • Good reset after the medieval streets and before dinner
  • Plenty of open space for photos with less crowd pressure

Most tourists miss: this is a better “do nothing for a bit” stop than trying to squeeze in one more museum.

17:45 – 18:25

Back to the hotel for a short rest

Head back to Center Hotel by public transport or a direct walk sequence, freshen up, and reset before dinner.

Walking rule check: no single walking stretch in this plan needs to exceed about 25–30 minutes.

18:45 – 20:15

Dinner — choose one of two group-friendly options

Option 1: Vegan Restoran V VEG
  • VEG beetroot ravioli, curry, roasted vegetable mains, vegan cake

Budget: €16–24 pp including a soft drink. Best all-round fit for Ketki and still interesting for everyone else.

Option 2: F-hoone VEG + NON-VEG
  • VEG marked vegan/vegetarian dishes, soups, salads, bowls
  • NON-VEG fish, chicken, and house comfort dishes

Budget: €18–25 pp including a soft drink. Good if the whole group wants wider choice without going upscale.

Local specialties to try today: black rye bread, smoked fish, mushroom dishes, kama-style sweets, and Estonian pastries.

20:15 – 21:00

Return to the hotel

Wrap up with a straightforward trip back to Center Hotel so the day finishes easily and close to transport.

If you still have energy: do a final short walk through the Rotermann area near your hotel rather than heading back uphill into Old Town again.

Smart tips

Hidden gems, local favorites & practical notes

Hidden gems & local favorites
  • St Catherine’s Passage is one of the best quiet medieval corners before tour groups thicken.
  • Telliskivi Creative City feels much more local than the postcard part of Old Town.
  • Linnahall is better for unwinding than trying to squeeze in one more paid attraction.
  • The upper floors of Balti Jaam Market are better for low-key browsing than the tourist-heavy Old Town souvenir lanes.
Practical tips tourists often miss
  • Cobblestones around Niguliste tänav and the cathedral hill can be slippery — grippy shoes help.
  • April weather in Tallinn can shift fast; light layers work better than one heavy jacket.
  • For a group of 4, market-style lunch is often faster than waiting for a full-table sit-down.
  • Ask servers directly for vegetarian labeling — Estonian menus are often clearly marked, especially at F-hoone and vegan spots.
Money-saving hacks • Food & drink
  • Use the bigger meal at lunch and keep dessert/light snacks flexible in the afternoon.
  • Tap water is drinkable, so skip bottled water and add one coffee/soft drink instead.
Money-saving hacks • Transport
  • A 24-hour public transport ticket costs €5.50 for non-residents and usually beats buying several single rides.
  • Use trams for the hotel ↔ Old Town / hotel ↔ Telliskivi legs, then walk inside compact areas instead of mixing short taxi hops.

Official details / buy info: tallinn.ee/en/pilet/public-transport-tickets-tallinn

Money-saving hacks • Attractions
  • Your tour already covers the best free hilltop viewpoints, so you do not need to pay for another tower just for views.
  • Choose atmosphere stops like Telliskivi Creative City and Linnahall instead of stacking ticketed sights.
Money-saving hacks • General
  • Skip the priciest Old Town souvenir lanes and browse creative district shops near Telliskivi instead.
  • Card payment is common almost everywhere, so you do not need much cash.
