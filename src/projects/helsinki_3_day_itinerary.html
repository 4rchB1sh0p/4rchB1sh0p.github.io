3 Days in Helsinki for Yash, Saurabh, Shruti & Ketki

A practical, click-anywhere, phone-friendly plan from 10:00 on 4 April to 17:00 on 6 April, built around walking + public transport from Innotelli Apartments.

49 spots covered Walking links kept short Veg-safe planning built in Budget target: ≤ €25 pp / meal Ends at hotel each day

Hotel: Sirrikuja 3 D, 00940 Helsinki, Finland

Group food strategy

Yash and Ketki are egg-vegetarian, so the itinerary leans on vegetarian-first cafés, vegan lunch options, and mixed restaurants with safe veg choices. Ask clearly for no fish sauce / no meat stock when in doubt.

Local favorites

Café Regatta for atmosphere, Hakaniemi Market Hall for less-touristy eating, Oodi Library as a true local everyday space, and the quieter corners of Suomenlinna after the first arrival zone.

Tourist tips most people miss

Wind off the sea makes Helsinki feel colder than the temperature looks. Keep a light windproof layer, a power bank, and one reusable water bottle per pair.

How to use this page

Tap any place name to open it in Google Maps. Each activity block is one card so the page works like a day-of-trip dashboard.

Transport · 2 money-saving hacks
  • Use HSL 24-hour AB day tickets on your heavy public-transport days instead of buying single rides again and again.
  • Pair nearby sights into walking clusters before boarding another tram. In the center, short hops add cost faster than distance.
Food · 2 money-saving hacks
  • Use lunch as the main paid meal. Helsinki lunch menus and buffets are usually the best value.
  • Split one dessert or pastry order in the middle of the day and keep drinks simple: tap water, tea, coffee, or house soft drinks.
Sightseeing · 2 money-saving hacks
  • Do not overpay for every interior. Some of Helsinki’s best experiences are outdoor: Senate Square, Esplanadi, Töölönlahti, Kaivopuisto, Suomenlinna sea walls.
  • Use one strong museum per cluster and enjoy the rest from outside when energy is low.
Souvenirs / shopping · 2 money-saving hacks
  • Buy edible gifts or small design items in Hakaniemi or market halls before touristy waterfront kiosks.
  • For Finnish chocolate and packaged gifts, city supermarkets and Fazer shops are usually better value than specialty souvenir stores.

Day 1 · Fri 4 Apr · Harbor, Old Town, Suomenlinna, Design South

Starts 10:00 from hotel · ends at hotel

10:00–10:45
Hotel → harbor start

Move: Leave Innotelli Apartments and take the metro toward the center. Target Helsinki Central Station / harbor area. A 24-hour HSL AB day ticket is the simplest move for this day.

Buy/details: HSL day tickets · Visitor guide · Zones

Practical tip: your address is outside the compact center, so the AB day ticket is usually the sweet spot for downtown + Suomenlinna, but check your exact route in HSL route planner before purchase.

10:45–11:30
Breakfast with a square view

Breakfast café: Café Engel, Helsinki VEG+NV · target spend €10–18 pp.

  • Veg picks for Yash/Ketki: egg breakfast, avocado toast if available, cinnamon bun, tea/coffee.
  • Non-veg picks for Saurabh/Shruti: breakfast plate or soup if running late.
  • Local specialty to try: korvapuusti (Finnish cinnamon bun).
  • After breakfast, you are already beside Senate Square, Helsinki Cathedral, Helsinki City Museum and Helsinki University.
11:30–13:15
Historic core walk

Walking cluster, all under 30 min links: Senate Square → Helsinki Cathedral → Helsinki City Museum → Helsinki University → Uspenski Cathedral.

  • Best photo trick: climb the cathedral steps, then turn back toward the square instead of only shooting the facade.
  • Hidden-gem angle: the streets behind Uspenski are calmer than the cathedral front and give a better red-brick skyline shot.
  • Keep interiors quick; save energy for the island later.
13:15–14:45
Harbor classics + lunch options

Walk: Market Square (Kauppatori), Helsinki → Old Market Hall, Helsinki → Havis Amanda Statue, Helsinki → optional quick look at SkyWheel Helsinki and Allas Sea Pool from outside.

Lunch option 1: Story Old Market Hall, Helsinki VEG+NV · €15–22 pp.

Lunch option 2: Zucchini, Helsinki VEG · €13–18 pp if the group wants a pure vegetarian lunch.

  • NV local specialty: creamy salmon soup (lohikeitto) at the market hall.
  • VEG move: seasonal soup + bread or Zucchini’s loaded lunch plate.
  • Money-saving hack: share one extra pastry instead of everyone ordering dessert here; save dessert for later.
14:45–17:45
Suomenlinna deep dive

Ferry: from harbor to Suomenlinna, Helsinki using the same HSL ticket.

  • Aim for scenic essentials rather than every museum: church area, walls, dock area, sea-facing bastions, tunnel-adjacent viewpoints.
  • Local-favorite trick: once crowds thin beyond the main ferry arrival zone, the island becomes much quieter.
  • Bring water and one snack from the market hall; island cafés can cost more.
  • The island is best for atmosphere and views, not rushing.

This is the biggest walking block of the trip, but it is worth keeping because your other two days are more stop-start and relaxed.

18:15–19:15
Design + south-coast unwind

Back on the mainland: use tram/short walk through Esplanadi Park, Helsinki → Helsinki Design District → Design Museum Helsinki → Punavuori District, Helsinki → Sinebrychoff Art Museum, Helsinki exterior/quick visit → Vanha Kirkkopuisto, Helsinki.

Chill finish before dinner: continue to Kaivopuisto Park, Helsinki or directly to Löyly Sauna, Helsinki waterfront for sunset mood.

19:30–21:15
Dinner choices + dessert

Dinner 1: Green Hippo Punavuori, Helsinki VEG+NV · €16–24 pp.

Dinner 2: Yes Yes Yes, Helsinki VEG · €20–25 pp.

Dessert: Fazer Café, Kluuvikatu 3 Helsinki VEG+NV · €6–12 pp.

  • Veg dishes: cauliflower / halloumi / mushroom plates at Yes Yes Yes; pasta or egg-friendly brunch-style options at Green Hippo.
  • Non-veg-friendly but still budget-safe: Green Hippo is the easier mixed group pick.
  • Local sweet to try: blueberry tart or Fazer chocolate pastry.
21:15–22:00
Return to hotel

End of day: metro/tram combo back to Sirrikuja 3 D, 00940 Helsinki, Finland.

Tourist tip most miss: keep a screenshot of your HSL ticket and the last metro direction in case the phone signal is patchy around the harbor crowd.

Day 2 · Sat 5 Apr · Töölö, museums, civic Helsinki, architecture

A scenic museum-and-neighborhood day with calmer pace

10:00–10:40
Hotel → Töölö district

Move: metro/tram to the west-center and start around Café Regatta, Helsinki / Töölö side. Today is your calm scenic architecture day with lots of short hops.

Pass idea: another 24-hour HSL AB ticket keeps buses/trams/ferries simple if you prefer zero friction.

10:40–11:20
Breakfast on the water

Breakfast café: Café Regatta, Helsinki VEG+NV · €8–15 pp.

  • Order: cinnamon bun, hot chocolate, coffee; simple egg-friendly items when available.
  • Local-favorite move: take photos outside first, then sit by the water instead of the tiny indoor queue zone.
  • Right after breakfast, tick off Sibelius Monument.
11:20–13:15
Töölö coastal cluster

Walking cluster: Sibelius Monument, Helsinki → Töölö District, Helsinki → Hietaniemi Beach, Helsinki → bus/short ride to Seurasaari Open-Air Museum, Helsinki.

  • Hidden gem: the shoreline segments between Regatta and Hietaniemi are often more enjoyable than the headline monument itself.
  • Seurasaari is where the trip briefly feels far from the capital without leaving the city.
  • Keep museum interiors short if weather is good; the value here is the open-air atmosphere.
13:15–14:15
Lunch picks near the center re-entry

Lunch option 1: Soup+More, Helsinki VEG+NV · €13–19 pp.

Lunch option 2: Lie Mi Kamppi, Helsinki VEG+NV · €15–22 pp.

  • Veg: tofu summer rolls, noodle bowl, or seasonal soup.
  • NV: tacos / chicken bowl if you want a break from Nordic food.
  • Money-saving hack: lunch menus are much better value than dinner in Helsinki; use lunch for your bigger meal.
14:15–17:30
Museum + civic-architecture loop

Very efficient central loop with short walks: Temppeliaukio Church, Helsinki → Natural History Museum Helsinki → Kamppi Chapel of Silence, Helsinki → Amos Rex, Helsinki → Helsinki Art Museum (HAM), Helsinki → Kiasma Museum of Contemporary Art, Helsinki → Oodi Library, Helsinki → Finlandia Hall, Helsinki → Töölönlahti, Helsinki → National Museum of Finland.

  • Most tourists rush Oodi; go up to the top floor windows for one of the nicest free indoor city views.
  • Kamppi Chapel is tiny, but it resets your energy in 10 minutes.
  • If museum fatigue hits, prioritize Temppeliaukio, Oodi, and the Töölönlahti walk.
17:30–18:45
Station classics + art fix

Finish the center cluster: Helsinki Central Station → Ateneum Art Museum, Helsinki exterior or quick visit.

Dessert stop: Dolce Italia, Helsinki VEG+NV · €6–11 pp for gelato / waffle.

19:00–21:00
Comfort-food dinner choices

Dinner 1: Ravintola Mountain, Helsinki VEG+NV · €15–23 pp.

Dinner 2: Ravintola Nepali, Helsinki VEG+NV · €14–22 pp.

  • This day intentionally leans South Asian for comfort after many museums.
  • Veg dishes: paneer, chana/chickpea curries, veg thali, naan; confirm no fish sauce or meat stock if needed.
  • NV dishes: chicken tikka or mixed Nepalese set.
  • Local trick: ask for the lunch-style set if the restaurant still has it running early evening.
21:00–21:45
Return to hotel

End of day: head back to Innotelli Apartments by metro.

Practical tip: if the group is tired, split museum interiors. Exterior ticks plus one strong highlight each is enough.

Day 3 · Sun 6 Apr · Kallio, Hakaniemi, gardens, Linnanmäki, zoo

Wrap by 17:00 at the hotel

10:00–10:40
Hotel → Kallio/Hakaniemi

Move: metro toward the center, start north-east of downtown. Today is neighborhood energy + markets + funfair + zoo, then back to the hotel by 17:00.

10:40–11:20
Breakfast in Kallio

Breakfast café: Green Hippo Kallio, Helsinki VEG+NV · €10–18 pp.

  • Veg: porridge, egg breakfast, toast, coffee.
  • NV: brunch bowl / added protein options.
  • Kallio is where Helsinki feels less polished and more lived-in.
11:20–12:45
Markets + neighborhood walk

Walk cluster: Kallio District, Helsinki → Hakaniemi Market Hall, Helsinki → Hakaniemi Market Square, Helsinki → Kaisaniemi Park, Helsinki → Botanical Garden Helsinki.

  • Hidden-gem tip: Hakaniemi is better than the harbor if you want locals doing normal-life shopping.
  • Best souvenir move: buy food gifts or simple design items here rather than the overpriced harbor stalls.
  • Botanical Garden is the softest, most relaxing stop of the day.
12:45–13:35
Lunch choices

Lunch option 1: Peace Kitchen, Helsinki VEG · €13.5–18 pp.

Lunch option 2: Thai Vegan Kitchen, Helsinki VEG · €14–20 pp.

  • This is your easiest full-veg day for Yash and Ketki.
  • Budget play: vegan lunch buffets in Helsinki often stretch further than dinner menus.
  • Group balance: non-veg eaters still get strong flavor here, so no one feels like they compromised.
13:35–15:00
Fun + east-harbor nature split

Afternoon route: tram/ride to Linnanmäki Amusement Park, Helsinki for the atmosphere and hill views, then continue to Helsinki Zoo (Korkeasaari), Helsinki for a lighter-paced final major stop.

  • You do not need full rides time at Linnanmäki; 30–45 minutes is enough to catch the vibe and views.
  • If the group feels zoo-fatigue, do a short scenic loop only; the goal is to cover the island and enjoy the waterfront setting.
15:00–16:00
Early dinner + dessert wrap-up

Dinner 1: Hakaniemi Market Hall, Helsinki stalls VEG+NV · €10–20 pp.

Dinner 2: Story, Helsinki fallback VEG+NV · €15–22 pp.

Dessert: Kuplavohvelibaari, Helsinki VEG+NV · €7–12 pp.

  • NV local specialty: fish plate or another salmon soup if someone wants one last Finnish comfort meal.
  • VEG: pie + soup, salad plates, waffle dessert.
  • Fastest endgame: eat near your return route instead of forcing one last detour.
16:00–17:00
Back to hotel by 5 pm

End of itinerary: metro back to Sirrikuja 3 D, 00940 Helsinki, Finland and arrive by 17:00.

This last day is intentionally flexible. If weather is poor, spend longer in the Botanical Garden and shorter at the zoo.
